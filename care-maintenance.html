Care and Maintenance
Glowing Forever

Easy to clean, cool to maintain—no sealer, no polishing required. Natural sheen and glow lasts forever.

Download Care Manual (PDF)

How to Clean | Routine Care

It is recommended to clean up any spills immediately, before they have a chance to dry. Use a mild detergent or cleaning agent (non-bleach and non-abrasive) to clean your Glowstone surface. Although Glowstone surfaces resist staining from liquids such as wine, vinegar, tea, lemon juice, and soda, as well as from fruits and vegetables, it's best to wipe up food and liquid spills as soon as possible.

For dried spills, use a non-abrasive cleaning pad such as a white 3M Scotch-Brite (or similar). Always use cutting boards and trivets when preparing food. While Glowstone surfaces are heat and scratch resistant, they are not heat- or scratch-proof. Avoid chopping or slicing food directly on the surface. To preserve the beauty of your Glowstone countertops, never place hot skillets or roasting pans directly on them—use trivets or heat-resistant pads instead.

Care Guidelines

Always use cutting boards and trivets when preparing food. Never place hot skillets or roasting pans directly on Glowstone—use trivets or heat-resistant pads instead. Avoid chopping or slicing food directly on the surface.

Casual exposure to alkaline solutions won't damage Glowstone surfaces. However, highly alkaline (high pH) cleaners—such as oven cleaners and concentrated bleach—are not recommended for use on Glowstone surfaces.

If grease from cooking is a concern, use a basic degreasing product to help loosen and remove it from the surface. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions for use.

Keep permanent markers and inks away from your surfaces. If they come into contact with the surface, clean it as outlined in the Routine Care section above. If the stain persists, moisten a cloth with Cif (or a comparable product) and gently rub it into the stain. Use warm water to remove any cleaner residue.

To maintain the beauty and durability of your Glowstone surface, avoid exposing it to harsh chemicals and solvents such as paint removers, furniture strippers, nail polish remover, bleach, bluing agents, and oil-based cleaners. Refrain from applying excessive force or pressure with heavy or sharp objects, as this can cause chipping. While Glowstone is scratch-resistant, we recommend using a chopping board to protect the surface from knives and other sharp tools—helping preserve its elegant appearance for years to come.

Dos and Don'ts

Recommendations for cleaning of quartz surface

Do

  • Always use a white cotton cloth for routine cleaning.
  • Opt for mild warm water for daily mopping.
  • For regular stains, use a combination of hot water, detergent, CIF, and acetone. Apply the cleaning mixture in a clockwise motion using a clean white cotton cloth.
  • Use soft microfiber mop pads and replace them regularly to prevent dirt buildup that could damage the stone.
  • Regular vacuuming and sweeping are recommended to keep quartz stone free from dust and dirt, which can lead to scratches.
  • Choose a neutral cleaner with a pH level between 5 and 8 for safe cleaning.
  • If the quartz flooring has grout lines, clean them frequently as they tend to collect more dust.
  • In high-traffic areas where scuffs, spills, and dirt accumulate daily, maintain a consistent cleaning routine for the floor.

Don't

  • Refrain from using harsh chemical cleaners like Harpic or other acid-based products.
  • Avoid using a dirty cloth for mopping, as it can lead to dust buildup.
  • Do not use commercial stain removers available in the market for surface cleaning.
  • Do not use ammonia-based cleaners, as they can cause the floor to lose its shine.
  • Avoid using rough materials such as scrapers, abrasive cleaners, steel wool, or scrub pads, which may scratch the stone surface.
